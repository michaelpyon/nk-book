## Q8. [First wife's family background?]
**Korean:** 평산군 석산면에서 제일부자이데 공산군이쫓아내서 38선이남으로 쪽겨나서 오막살이에서살았음
**Translation:** "[They] were the wealthiest family in Seoksan-myeon, Pyeongsan-gun. The communist forces drove them out, and they were pushed south of the 38th parallel, where they lived in a shack."

**Note:** This describes the first wife's family (Kim Ok-i's family), not grandfather's own family. They were wealthy landowners targeted by communist land reform.

**Historical context:**
- **Pyeongsan-gun (평산군):** County in Hwanghae Province, North Korea, directly on the 38th parallel.
- **Seoksan-myeon (석산면):** Township within Pyeongsan-gun.
- Wealthy landowners were primary targets of North Korean communist land reform (1946 onward).
- "오막살이" (shack-dwelling): From wealthiest in the township to a makeshift shelter after fleeing south.
**Confidence:** High

## Q9. [How did first wife die?]
**Korean:** 북한엔 약도업고 병원도 있어도약이업는데 빠갱이가 네남편 데려오라고 고문당하고결국 주었음 내가그놈들하고 많이 싸워서 나는잡히면 죽는놈이니까
**Translation:** "In North Korea there was no medicine. Even if there were hospitals, they had no medicine. The communists (빠갱이) harassed [her], demanding 'Bring your husband,' and in the end she died. I fought with those bastards a lot, so if I had been caught, I was a dead man."

**Note:** The most compressed and typo-heavy answer. Current best reading: his first wife (Kim Ok-i) was harassed by communists trying to get to him. There was no medicine available. She died, likely from illness compounded by the stress of persecution and lack of medical care. Not direct violence. The harassment and the absence of medicine together created the conditions.

**NEEDS FOLLOW-UP.** This is the only answer that should not be locked into polished narrative prose without clarification. Suggested follow-up: confirm this refers to Kim Ok-i, clarify the nature of the harassment, and whether her death was from illness.

**빠갱이 (ppalgaengi):** Derogatory term for communists, literally "reds." Common among Korean War generation.
**Confidence:** LOW — needs clarification before manuscript use

## Q10. [How did you meet grandmother?]
**Korean:** 1957년에 여등포에서 하숙하며 미군부대에 다니는데 주인할머니가 중매해서 나같은 피난민에게 오갔다는사람이라 고마워서
**Translation:** "In 1957, I was boarding in Yeongdeungpo and working at a US military base. The landlady arranged the match. I was grateful because she was someone willing to come to a refugee like me."

**Key details:**
- **Yeongdeungpo (영등포):** District in Seoul. Major area for refugees and US military workers.
- **중매 (jungmae):** Traditional arranged matchmaking.
- He married his second wife (Michael's grandmother) around 1957.
- The emotional weight: a penniless refugee, grateful anyone would accept him.
- **Possible follow-up:** Did he tell his second wife the full story of Kim Ok-i?
**Confidence:** High

## Q12. [Tell me about Hong Seung-guk]
**Korean:** 홍승국은 나와 같은직장에있는데 결혼을한대서 집안에아무도업다해서 결혼피로연을 내가준비해서 했드니 그때부터 나를아버지라부르고 월남에 기술자로 갔다가 미국에서 신세계부동산이고 큰부도산회서하다가 흑이청년으도와주다 초에맞아 하늘나라에갔다 1975년8월15일 그래서 내가10년을 기일날엔꼭묘지에갔었다
**Translation:** "Hong Seung-guk worked at the same place as me. When he said he was getting married and had no family, I prepared the wedding reception for him. From then on, he called me 'Father.' He went to Vietnam as a technician, then came to America and worked in real estate (신세계부동산, 'Sinsekai Real Estate'), running a large real estate company. While helping a Black youth, he was shot and went to heaven. August 15, 1975. So for 10 years, I always visited his grave on the anniversary."

**Key details:**
- Grandfather stepped in as surrogate father for a coworker with no family.
- Hong went to Vietnam as a Korean contract technician, then emigrated to America.
- Murdered August 15, 1975 (also Korean Liberation Day, 광복절).
- Grandfather visited his grave every anniversary for 10 years.
**Confidence:** High

## Q13. [Who was most important to your career?]
**Korean:** 나를제도교육받게 보내준 조기행씨인데 군제대후엔열락이 끈켰는데 그분덕에내가제도사로 도안사로 엔지니아로일을하다 여기까지와서 휴즈사엔지니어로까지 일하고 65세에 은퇴했다 참고마운분이다
**Translation:** "Jo Gi-haeng was the person who sent me to get technical drafting education. After military discharge, we lost contact. But thanks to him, I worked as a draftsman, a designer, and then an engineer. I came all the way here [to America] and worked as an engineer at Hughes [Aircraft Company] until I retired at 65. He is someone I am truly grateful to."

**Career path:** draftsman (제도사) → designer (도안사) → engineer (엔지니어) → Hughes Aircraft Company (LA) → retired at 65.
**Confidence:** High
