## THE 13 QUESTIONS THAT REMAIN

**Instructions:** Number answers to match questions. Take your time on 5, 6, 8, 10, 11, 12, 13. Don't rush those.

### BLOCK 1: The facts we still don't have (quick)

**1. 할아버지, 몇 년도에 태어나셨어요?**
*(What year were you born?)*
→ The book never states it directly. Chapter 1 says you turned 16 at Liberation (August 1945), which puts birth around 1929. Just confirm the year.

**2. 어머니 성함이 안씨라고만 적어주셨는데, 성함 전체를 혹시 기억하시나요?**
*(You wrote your mother's family name was Ahn. Do you remember her full name?)*
→ She died saving a drowning woman when you were 10. If you don't remember, that's the answer.

**3. 어머니 성함이 안씨라고만 적어주셨는데, 성함 전체를 혹시 기억하시나요?**
*(You said your grandmother was from the Jo family. Do you remember her given name?)*

### BLOCK 2: Ok-i's death — the one thing the book doesn't fully say

**4. 책에 옥이 씨가 스물여덟에 돌아가셨다고 쓰셨는데, 무슨 병이었는지 아세요? 공산군이 정확히 어떻게 했어요?**
*(The book says Ok-i died at 28. Do you know what illness it was? What exactly did the communists do to her?)*
→ The book says she died waiting. Your daughter told you at the hotel in 2005. But we don't know: illness, what the harassment looked like, where you were when it happened. If the detail is too painful, we record that it is too painful.

---

### BLOCK 3: What the book says but can't feel

**5. 탈출하기 전날 밤, 옥이 씨 품에서 마지막으로 잤을 때 무슨 생각이 들었어요?**
*(The night before you left — when you held Ok-i for the last time — what were you thinking?)*
→ The book describes what you said: "Fifteen days." But not what you felt holding a woman eight months pregnant, knowing you might never see her again.

**6. 2005년에 호텔에서 딸이 "어머니가 아버지 보고 싶어하다가 병이 나셨어요"라고 했을 때, 그 순간이 어땠어요?**
*(In 2005, at the hotel, when your daughter told you Ok-i missed you so much she got sick — what was that moment like?)*
→ The book records the words. It doesn't say what happened inside you when you heard them.

**7. 2005년에 북한에서 가족을 만났을 때 가장 먼저 무슨 말을 했어요? 무슨 생각이 들었어요?**
*(When you met your family in North Korea in 2005 — after 54 years — what were the first words? What went through your mind?)*
→ The book has the dialogue. But what it felt like to collide with your sister after 54 years — that's what the spoken answer carries.

---

### BLOCK 4: Things the book never addresses

**8. 휴즈에서 어떤 프로젝트에서 일하셨어요? 헬리콥터요? 미사일이요? 뭘 설계하셨어요?**
*(At Hughes — what projects? Helicopters? Missiles? What did you actually design?)*
→ 17.5 years at one of America's most important Cold War defense companies. The book says you drew things. What things? This could be remarkable.

**9. 두 번째 부인, 할머니는 북한 이야기를 다 알고 계셨나요? 옥이 씨 이야기도요?**
*(Did your second wife — Michael's grandmother — know your full story? About Ok-i? About the daughter in North Korea?)*
→ The book is silent on this. Did you carry this alone for the whole marriage? Did she know there was a daughter on the other side? This is a question about what two people share across a long life together.

**10. 폐암 오진 받았을 때, 암이라는 말 들었을 때 어떤 생각이 드셨어요? 그리고 아니라는 거 알았을 때는요?**
*(When they told you it was lung cancer — what did you think? And when they said it was wrong — what was that like?)*
→ A man who survived the Korean War, escaped on a boat, watched his first wife die, lost his surrogate son, crossed the world — then gets told he has cancer, then gets told he doesn't. What does that do to you?

**11. 민식이 생각이 지금도 나세요?**
*(Do you still think about Minsik?)*
→ The book ends with you going to his grave nearly every week, standing there without speaking. Just: does the grief still live in you the same way?

---

### BLOCK 5: The closing questions

**12. 살면서 가장 후회되는 게 뭐예요?**
*(What is your biggest regret?)*
→ The book already gives one answer: Huiseong's phone call. "If I could take back one moment in my life, one single decision, it would be that phone call." But there may be others. Ask anyway — let him say it out loud.

**13. 손자들한테 꼭 알려주고 싶은 게 있으면 뭐예요?**
*(If there's one thing you want your grandchildren to know, what is it?)*
→ The book's answer is scattered across every chapter. This question makes him say it directly. It becomes the manuscript's closing line.
