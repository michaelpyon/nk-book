# Round 2 Questions for Grandfather
**Purpose:** Fill gaps from Round 1, resolve conflicts, deepen the story
**Instructions:** Number the answers to match the questions, like last time.
**Note:** Some questions reference his earlier answers. That's intentional. It shows we're listening.
**Time estimate:** 45-60 minutes. Don't rush 6-8, 19-21, or 25-27.

---

## IDENTITY (still missing)

1. 할아버지 성함이 뭐예요? 본인 이름이요.
*(What is YOUR name? Your own full name.)*
→ We have his father, grandfather, and first wife's name. Not his.

2. 몇 년도에 태어나셨어요?
*(What year were you born?)*

3. 어머니 성함이 안씨라고만 말씀하셨는데, 성함 전체를 혹시 기억하시나요?
*(You said your mother's family name was Ahn. Do you remember her full name?)*

---

## GEOGRAPHY (resolving a conflict)

4. 할아버지는 영백군에서 사셨다고 하셨는데, 옥이 씨 가족은 평산군 석산면이라고 하셨어요. 할아버지 가족은 어디서 사셨어요? 영백군 어디요?
*(You mentioned living in Yŏngbaek-gun, but Ok-i's family was from Seoksan-myeon, Pyeongsan-gun. Where exactly did YOUR family live? Where in Yŏngbaek-gun?)*
→ The video says Yŏngbaek-gun. The interview says Pyeongsan-gun. These are different places. We need to know which was his family vs. her family.

5. 북한에서 살 때 집이 어떻게 생겼었어요? 동네가 어떤 데였어요?
*(What did your house look like in North Korea? What was the neighborhood like?)*
→ Scene-setting for the book. Oral history best practice: ground the story in physical details before the disruption.

---

## LIFE BEFORE THE WAR

6. 전쟁 나기 전에 매일 뭘 하셨어요? 보통 하루가 어땠어요?
*(Before the war, what did you do every day? What was a normal day like?)*
→ The reader needs to know what "normal" was before it was destroyed.

7. 옥이 씨랑 어떻게 만나셨어요? 중매였어요? 연애였어요?
*(How did you and Ok-i meet? Was it arranged or did you fall in love?)*

8. 옥이 씨는 어떤 사람이었어요? 성격이 어땠어요?
*(What kind of person was Ok-i? What was her personality like?)*
→ She's a central figure in the story but we know almost nothing about who she was.

---

## FIRST WIFE'S DEATH (Q9 follow-up, be gentle)

9. 아까 옥이 씨가 공산군한테 괴롭힘을 당했다고 하셨는데, 좀 더 자세히 말씀해 주실 수 있으세요? 무슨 병이 있었어요? 공산군이 뭘 했어요?
*(You said Ok-i was harassed by the communists. Can you tell me a little more? Was she ill? What did the communists do?)*

10. 옥이 씨가 돌아가셨을 때 할아버지는 어디 계셨어요? 소식을 어떻게 들으셨어요?
*(Where were you when Ok-i passed away? How did you hear the news?)*

## THE ESCAPE

11. 배를 타고 남한으로 오셨다고 하셨는데, 그 배에 누구랑 같이 탔어요? 가족이랑 같이요?
*(You said you came south by boat. Who was on the boat with you? Were you with family?)*
→ Video testimony confirms boat + Incheon arrival. Now we need the scene.

12. 배 타기 전날 밤에 뭘 하셨어요? 떠나기 전에 누구한테 작별인사를 하셨어요?
*(What did you do the night before you got on the boat? Did you say goodbye to anyone before leaving?)*
→ This is a book moment. The last night before everything changed.

13. 인천에 도착해서 처음에 어떻게 하셨어요? 김포에 어떻게 가셨어요?
*(When you arrived in Incheon, what did you do first? How did you get to Gimpo?)*
→ Video says Incheon then Gimpo. Fill the gap.

## MILITARY

14. 군대에 언제 들어가셨어요? 몇 년도요? 왜 입대하셨어요?
*(When did you join the military? What year? Why did you enlist?)*

15. 군대에서 2개월 있었다고 하셨는데, 왜 그렇게 짧았어요?
*(You said you were in the military for 2 months. Why was it so short?)*
→ Wait. Q6 was about the baby dying at 2 months, not military. Need to check. If he did serve briefly, this question stands. If not, skip. **Ask whoever is conducting the interview to use judgment here.**

## COMING TO AMERICA

19. 미국에 몇 년도에 오셨어요?
*(What year did you come to America?)*

20. 어떻게 미국에 오게 되셨어요? 누가 도와줬어요?
*(How did you end up coming to America? Did anyone help you?)*

21. 미국에 처음 도착했을 때 영어를 할 줄 아셨어요?
*(When you first arrived in America, could you speak English?)*

22. 미국에 와서 가장 힘들었던 게 뭐였어요? 가장 놀랐던 건요?
*(What was the hardest thing in America? What surprised you the most?)*

## CAREER

23. 조기행 씨한테 제도교육을 받았다고 하셨는데, 그 교육을 어디서 받으셨어요? 한국에서요 미국에서요?
*(You said Jo Gi-haeng sent you for drafting training. Where was that training? Korea or America?)*

24. 휴즈사에서 어떤 일을 하셨어요? 무슨 프로젝트에서 일하셨어요?
*(What did you do at Hughes? What kind of projects?)*
→ Hughes Aircraft made satellites, missiles, helicopters. The specifics matter for the book.

## FAMILY LEFT BEHIND

25. 북한에 남은 가족이 있어요? 형제자매요?
*(Do you have family left in North Korea? Brothers, sisters?)*

26. 이산가족 상봉 신청을 하신 적이 있으세요?
*(Did you ever apply for the separated families reunion program?)*
→ The South Korean government runs a lottery for family reunification meetings. Many survivors applied for decades. This is a significant question.

27. 북한에 남은 가족 소식을 들은 적 있으세요? 어떤 경로로요?
*(Have you ever heard any news about family in North Korea? Through what channel?)*

## THE MAPS

28. 비디오에서 북한 지도를 보여주셨는데, 그 지도는 어디서 구하셨어요? 왜 그걸 가지고 계세요?
*(In the video, you showed maps of North Korea. Where did you get those maps? Why do you keep them?)*
→ The video shows large, heavily worn administrative maps. These are artifacts. They tell a story of someone who never stopped looking.

## REFLECTION

30. 살면서 가장 후회되는 게 뭐예요?
*(What is your biggest regret in life?)*

31. 손자들한테 꼭 알려주고 싶은 게 있으면 뭐예요?
*(If there's one thing you want your grandchildren to know, what is it?)*

32. 다시 그때로 돌아갈 수 있다면, 뭘 다르게 하시겠어요?
*(If you could go back to that time, what would you do differently?)*

**Total: 32 questions**

**Interview tips:**
- Record audio if possible. His voice is part of the story.
- Let him talk. If a question opens a 10-minute story, don't cut him off. Those tangents are often the best material.
- Questions 9-10 and 25-27 are the heaviest. Watch his body language. If he needs a break, take one.
- If he starts talking about something not on this list, let him. Write down what question it relates to after.
- The maps (Q28) and artifacts (Q29) might trigger deep memories. Have a camera ready.
